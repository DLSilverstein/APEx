Above the Dew Point (60.4°C): All water is in the gas phase (superheated). The vapor composition is fixed at 20% H₂O.

Below the Dew Point: Liquid water forms. The vapor phase is "saturated" – its H₂O content is the maximum possible at that temperature. As you cool, more water condenses from the vapor.
