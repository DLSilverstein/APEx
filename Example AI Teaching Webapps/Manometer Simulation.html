Manometer Physics

Compare a standard U-Tube Manometer vs. an Inclined Manometer.
Assume atmospheric pressure (Patm) is 760 mmHg.

1. U-Tube Manometer

Patm = 760 mmHg

Δh = 0 mm

760 mmHg
760 mmHg (Atm) 800 mmHg

Physics: In a U-tube, the fluid moves down on the high-pressure side and up on the low-pressure side. The total height difference (h) equals the pressure difference in mmHg.
h = P_gas - P_atm

2. Inclined Manometer

Angle (θ) = 30°

Length (L) = 0 mm

760 mmHg
760 mmHg 800 mmHg

Physics: The liquid moves a distance L along the incline to achieve a vertical height h.
L = h / sin(θ). At 30°, the liquid moves 2mm for every 1mm of pressure head. This creates higher sensitivity.
