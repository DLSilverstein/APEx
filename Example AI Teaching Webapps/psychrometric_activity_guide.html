1. Introduction: The Geometry of Air

A psychrometric chart is a graphical representation of the thermodynamic state of a mixture of dry air and water vapor. Under Gibbs' Phase Rule, a mixture of two gases can be completely specified by three independent intensive properties. If we fix the total pressure ($P$), we can plot any state point on a two-dimensional coordinate space using any two other independent variables.

Task 1 Orienting Yourself on the Grid

Open the Psychrometric Chart Simulator. Set the elevation to 0 m (Sea Level) and toggle the units to Metric (SI). Locate the primary axes and boundary curves of the interactive plot, and answer the following:

b. What property is mapped to the vertical axis on the right side of the plot (Ordinate)? State its variable symbol and unit. Why is it scaled in g/kg of dry air instead of simply kg/kg?

c. Hover your cursor along the far-left boundary curve where Relative Humidity is exactly 100%. What is this curve called, and what physical transition does it represent?
